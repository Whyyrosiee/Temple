About the Temple

Hemadpanthi Style Architecture

An Ancient Seat of Devotion

Nestled in the tranquil village of Kokamthan near Kopargaon in Ahmednagar district, this revered temple stands as a timeless testament to Maharashtra's rich spiritual and architectural heritage. Built in the celebrated Hemadpanthi style — originating under the patronage of the Yadava dynasty in the 12th–13th centuries — the temple reflects the unique genius of Minister Hemadri Pandit.

What makes this temple extraordinary is the rare consecration of both Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu together — the two supreme manifestations of the divine, coexisting in sacred harmony, drawing devotees from across Kopargaon, Nashik, and the Godavari belt.

The black basalt stone construction, fitted without mortar using ancient tenon-and-mortise joints, has withstood centuries of seasons. The temple's serrated façades, pillared mandapa, and crowned shikhara speak of an era when craftsmanship was itself a form of worship.

Built in Stone & Spirit

Hemadpanthi Architecture

The Hemadpanthi style — born in 13th-century Deccan — is one of Maharashtra's most revered architectural traditions. Named after the visionary minister Hemadri Pandit of the Yadava court, it speaks a language of stone that needs no binding, no mortar — only precision and devotion.

01

Black Basalt Construction

Locally sourced Deccan basalt, fitted with extraordinary precision using tenon-and-mortise joints — no mortar, no cement. The stone itself becomes the prayer, enduring centuries without yielding.

02

Serrated Façades

Distinctive sawtooth projections on the outer walls create ever-shifting patterns of light and shadow throughout the day — a living, breathing surface that transforms with the sun.

03

Star-Shaped Plan

The star-shaped (stellate) ground plan, characteristic of Yadava-era temples, creates a sense of dynamic radiance — the sacred geometry of a rising sun rendered in stone.

04

Intricate Stone Carvings

Pillars, ceilings and walls bear carved depictions of deities, mythological scenes, lotus motifs, and geometric patterns — each chisel stroke a meditation, each form a verse of devotion.

05

Pillared Mandapa

The spacious columned hall (mandapa) serves as the gathering ground for devotees — where song, prayer and community converge beneath a canopy of sculpted stone.

06

Crowned Shikhara

The rising tower (shikhara), tiered and adorned, culminates in a kalasha pinnacle that reaches towards the infinite — a stone finger pointing beyond itself to the sacred.

Celebrations of the Sacred

Festivals & Events

🔱

Mahashivratri

The grand night of Lord Shiva, celebrated with all-night vigils, Rudrabhishek, and thousands of devotees journeying to offer their prayers. The most sacred night at this temple.

🪷

Ekadashi

Observed twice monthly in honour of Lord Vishnu. Devotees fast and attend special evening discourses and bhajans through the night.

🎆

Diwali & Tripurari Purnima

The temple is illuminated with oil lamps on these auspicious evenings, casting a warm golden glow on the ancient stone — a sight that connects worshippers to centuries past.

🌸

Ram Navami & Hanuman Jayanti

Celebrated with processions, kirtan and special prasad distribution. The community gathers in a spirit of collective devotion and festivity.

🥁

Navaratri

Nine nights of divine celebration with Garba, devotional music and Devi puja. The temple courtyard comes alive with song and the rhythm of shared worship.

☀️

Somavara Vrat (Mondays)

Every Monday is special — devotees observe fasts and offer bilva leaves to Lord Shiva. The morning Abhishek on Mondays draws large numbers of faithful from Kopargaon and surrounding villages.

🚗 Getting Here

By Road:
Kokamthan is situated approximately 5–7 km from Kopargaon city center. Regular auto-rickshaws and local buses connect Kopargaon to the village. The temple is easily reachable from Shirdi (≈25 km) and Nashik (≈90 km).

By Train:
The nearest major railway station is Kopargaon Railway Station, which is well-connected to Nashik, Pune, Mumbai and Aurangabad. From the station, take a local auto or taxi to Kokamthan village.

By Air:
The closest airport is Shirdi Airport (SAG), approximately 30 km away. Cabs are readily available from the airport.

From Shirdi:
Many pilgrims visiting Shirdi Sai Baba Temple combine this darshan, as Kokamthan lies on the Shirdi–Kopargaon route.
